Themes

  1. Addiction - Drugs and alcohol are a crutch to many individuals in the community.  Between the issue of poor awareness and minimal health education, addiction in the community has gotten out of control.  Despite the health department's efforts to begin a Needle Exchange Program, there has been a big push to make the community work through the issue on its own.  Local individuals are against the idea of helping addicts accomplish their goal in a safer manner.  This lack of education about the issue is discouraging for healthcare providers in the area because of the growing issue of addiction.
    Problem Resolution - This problem can be helped with the proper public education; the health department can hold health fairs that provide information about the addiction problem in Lawrence County and how it can be helped.  This may cause the public to see the positive aspects of the Needle Exchange Program.  In addition, the Addiction Recovery Care (ARC) facility provides a Christian-based approach to leaving the path of addiction.  This rehabilitation center is a good place for addicts to visit and seek help.
  2. Obesity & Diabetes - Many local individuals are not well-educated in medical matters.  Since this is the case, most people live the way they want and consume whatever they feel like eating.  This disregard for a healthy lifestyle leads to weight problems and poor management of blood sugar.  Since there are several fast food restaurants in the area and very few exercise opportunities, many individuals seek comfort in food and a sedentary lifestyle.
    Problem Resolution - Once again, the health department can hold health fairs that provide the public with information about obesity and diabetes.  Local people need to be educated about how the usual sedentary lifestyle is harmful to their health and will lead to complications in the future.  In addition, individuals should find a general practitioner that can discuss lifestyle changes to help improve their weight problem.  Local gyms can be utilized to work off excess weight.  Individuals can buy fresh food at a grocery store rather than at a restaurant.
  3. Environmental Hazards - Several locations in the county produce harmful waste or litter the surrounding areas.  For example, many local tire yards burn tires rather than disposing of them in the proper manner; in addition, drug paraphernalia can be found in the streets of Louisa, which creates an unsafe environment for people to walk.  These two hazards pose risks to many local peoples' health.
    Problem Resolution - Many of these problems can be solved with proper education and providing the right resources.  The health department can provide much of this education to those who put pullutants into the air of the community.  Recovery programs can be enforced to help the issue of addiction in the community.  Once the education has been enforced, a group should volunteer to clean up the mess that has been left as a result of the previous lack of education.  Needles must be cleared from the streets and tires should be disposed of properly.  This will make the community healthier and safer.
